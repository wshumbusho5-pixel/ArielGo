## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### The Opportunity
The US laundry and dry cleaning market is a **$10+ billion industry** that remains highly fragmented with no dominant national player. Previous attempts (Washio, Prim) failed due to poor unit economics and premature scaling. We will succeed by:

1. **Starting lean** as a service provider to perfect operations
2. **Achieving profitability** at the unit level before scaling
3. **Building the platform** only after proving demand
4. **Expanding methodically** to cities with proven playbooks

### The Model
**Phase 1 (Months 1-6)**: Solo operator in U-District → Validate demand & unit economics
**Phase 2 (Months 6-12)**: Hire 3-5 operators → Build systems & processes
**Phase 3 (Year 2)**: Launch marketplace platform → Onboard independent providers
**Phase 4 (Year 3-5)**: Multi-city expansion → Scale to 50-100 cities

## BUSINESS MODEL

### Revenue Streams

**Phase 1-2: Direct Service Provider**
- Wash & fold per pound: $1.75/lb
- Wash & fold per bag: $28/bag (standard 24hr)
- Express same-day: $38/bag
- Rush 4-hour: $45/bag
- Monthly subscriptions: $99 (4 bags/month)
- Add-ons: Dry cleaning, alterations, special care

**Phase 3-4: Marketplace Platform**
- Commission on transactions: 20-25%
- Subscription fees from providers: $99-299/month (for tools/leads)
- Premium placement fees: $200-500/month
- Bulk commercial accounts: Custom pricing
- White-label solutions for laundromats: Licensing fees

### Pricing Strategy

**Penetration Pricing (Year 1):**
- Price 15-20% below competitors
- Focus on volume and market share
- Goal: 100+ regular customers

**Value Pricing (Year 2-3):**
- Match market rates as quality/speed proven
- Premium for speed (same-day, rush)
- Subscription discounts for retention

**Dynamic Pricing (Year 4+):**
- Algorithm-based pricing by demand/supply
- Surge pricing for peak times (optional)
- Volume discounts for high-frequency users

## RISKS & MITIGATION

### Market Risks

**Risk 1: Low demand / customers won't pay**
- **Mitigation**: Start in dense U-District, high validation market. If 50+ customers in 3 months, demand proven.
- **Pivot**: Adjust pricing, add services (dry cleaning, alterations)

**Risk 2: Competitors respond aggressively (price war)**
- **Mitigation**: Focus on differentiation (speed, service, student focus), not just price. Build brand loyalty.
- **Advantage**: We're smaller, can pivot faster

**Risk 3: Market saturation**
- **Mitigation**: Fragmented $10B market, plenty of room. Focus on underserved segments.

### Operational Risks

**Risk 4: Can't maintain quality at scale**
- **Mitigation**: Strict provider vetting, ratings system, quality assurance team
- **Plan**: Invest in training, SOPs, support infrastructure

**Risk 5: Provider churn (supply side)**
- **Mitigation**: Fair revenue share, support tools, community building
- **Plan**: Always recruit 2x needed providers

**Risk 6: Logistics complexity (routing, scheduling)**
- **Mitigation**: Invest in routing software early, learn from food delivery playbooks
- **Plan**: Partner with logistics optimization platforms

### Financial Risks

**Risk 7: Burn too much cash, run out of runway**
- **Mitigation**: Bootstrap to profitability first, raise only when needed
- **Plan**: Maintain 12-18 months runway at all times

**Risk 8: Unit economics don't work at scale**
- **Mitigation**: Validate in Phase 1-2 before scaling
- **Plan**: Regular cohort analysis, ruthless focus on contribution margin

### Strategic Risks

**Risk 9: Platform disintermediation (providers go direct)**
- **Mitigation**: Build strong brand, provide value beyond leads (software, insurance, support)
- **Plan**: Make platform indispensable through network effects

**Risk 10: Regulatory issues (labor laws, licensing)**
- **Mitigation**: Providers are independent contractors, not employees. Follow Uber/DoorDash model.
- **Plan**: Legal counsel, compliance team as we scale
